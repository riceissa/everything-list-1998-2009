William S. Cooper says: "The absolutist outlook has it that if a logic is
valid at all it is valid period. A sound logic is completely sound
everywhere and for everyone, no exceptions! For absolutist logicians a
logical truth is regarded as 'true in all possible worlds', making logical
laws constant, timeless and universal."
 
Where do the laws of logic come from? he asks the absolutist.
