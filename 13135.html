Yes, I think there's a grain of truth in that. The integers aren't *just out there*. By Peano's, or anyone else's, axioms they are generated as needed. We don't want to run out so we (except Torgny) always allow one more, but we never need the whole set at once until we want to make diagonalization arguments.
