    You seem to take metaphysics to be an absolutist theory. Maybe Tom does too. But I think of metaphysics to be the interpretation we put on top of our mathematical theories, e.g. Bohm's pilot wave and Feynman's multiple particle paths are two different metaphysics we can use to explain what the formalism of quantum mechanics refers to. But we're *less* certain about them than about the formalism. In fact they don't even matter in applications. Their usefulness, if they have any, is in suggesting extensions to the theory.

    Brent Meeker
