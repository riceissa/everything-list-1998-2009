I don't understand "assumptions about logic and math"? We don't need to make assumptions about them
because they are rules we made up to keep us from reaching self-contradictions when making long
complex inferences. They are rules about propositions and inferences. The propositions may be
about an observation like "a species that used this kind of reasoning survived more frequently than
those who used that kind." I might need logic to make further inferences, but I don't need
assumptions about logic to understand it.
