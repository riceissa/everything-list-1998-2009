This is what I was trying to say. But I was also trying to point to
what the "more" is, the truth that discovery is discovering, and ask
why couldn't that truth be the same truth. But with mathematics we are
exploring it largely in a constructive way, and in science we are
exploring in an empirical way. That doesn't mean that they are
studying two different things, just two blind men exploring the same
elephant. Actually empirical science is exploring the elephant
empirically by feeling it, and math is exploring by trying to construct
elephants from scratch and seeing if they resemble real elephants. And
by trying to construct elephants starting from different sets of axioms
and inference rules, hopefully we find out something about the nature
of how reality works, because the elephant isn't really what we're
interested in but what makes up the elephant, because hopefully that's
what makes up everything else.
