> Let's start with this. The "Schmidhuber plenitude" is the universal
> program, computing all possible *computable* universes. This is an
> entirely different scheme than Tegmark's, which is based on formal
> logic. So far, only vague analogies have been offered to link the

They're not vague analogies. There are two ways to link the two
plenitudes, as I had pointed out before, but will try to make more
explicit now. Each member of the Tegmark plenitude is characterised by
a finite set of axioms (given as a symbol strings), and a finite set
of transition rules (the rules of logic). These may be encoded as a
Schmidhuber bitstring as an interpreter describing how to generate all
theorems given the axiom set, and the axiom set itself. It doesn't
matter what encoding is chosen, the compiler theorem ensures that
everything is self consistent. As in my previous post this morning,
this induces a Universal measure on the Tegmark plenitude.

Now the other link is that a UTM operating on bitstrings is in itself
a self-consistent mathematical system.

On the surface, these two links would imply that each plenitude is a
proper subset of each other. On the surface...

My own conviction is that the former way of linking the two systems is
the relevant one, and that the two systems aren't in fact
isomorphic. (ie that Tegmark \subset Schmidhuber). However, strong
that conviction is though, I am of course amenable to reasonable
argument. I also believe that the Tegmark plenitude is the important
"core" of the Schmidhuber from an anthropic perspective - ie
Schmidhuber universes containing SASes will be in most cases
arbitrarily close to the Tegmark one. Sorry for thowing in another
analogy, but its a bit like the Feynman path integral - the measure of
quantum trajectories will peak around the classical trajectory - in
this case the anthropic universes will peak around the Tegmark ensemble.
