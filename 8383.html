This leads to the virtual reality argument that David Deutsch
pursues. The 3D brain presumably notices that its 2D environment is
incompatible with its existence in some manner, and thus reasons it
must be instantiated in another (higher dimensional) environment.

But of course, only if the 2D physics is not believable. The question
is - can a 2D physics believably generate an observer?
