Ok, so you want to solve the "hard problem" right at the beginning by
taking conscious thoughts as the basic elements of your ontology. Then
they could be something like Bertrand Russell's neutral monads. If you
string them into sequences according to a viewpoint the sequence
constitutes a person's stream of consciousness. If you note that some
such viewpoints see the same thing from different pespectives
(intersubjective agreement), you create a model of external space which
explains the different viewpoints. I think Russell supposed that monads
corresponded to all actual events. But you could suppose that all
possible (logically consistent) monads exist and then try to solve the
white rabbit problem, why do some things happen and others don't (at
least apparently).
