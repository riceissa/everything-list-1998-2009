Sorry, but I'm not sampling my own age independently. The probability
that I see myself as age 36 years, given that I'm 36 years old is
1. It is zero for any other age. The probability that I see JM at age
36, given that I am 36 years old is a value given by the distribution
of observer moments that you talk about. That is independent sampling.

Your use of Bayesian enquiry is ridiculous, because you cannot sample
your own age independently.
