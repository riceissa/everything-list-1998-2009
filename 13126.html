Thanks for a clear mind, Bruno. But isn't it obvious? We can "know" about what we don't know ONLY if we do know 'about it'. Copernicus did not know that he does not know radioactivity. Aristotle did not denigrate the linearity of QM because he did not know these items.

My 'firm' knowledge of my ignorance stems from earlier memory: I know (remember) not having learnt many things I would have needed later on (by laziness or lack of interest). Nowadays I find myself exposed to other items of my ignorance and feel "lazy" to start studying things I did not study at 21. Don't even have the time (?) and tutor (school) - plus: I have a suspicious (violent?) mind and start arguing instead of learning.
So I stay stupid (but happily so).
