This is a very interesting point and highlights the false distinction that
many people have made between the hardware and the software.

>From memory, Kolmogorov states that the complexity in a system 'X' is given
by the length of the shortest possible UTM (computer) program required to
generate that system. As this depends on the complexity of the UTM (it could
have the system 'X' in its BIOS and be programmed to replicate it when you
enter the command 'bumblebee X') as much as of the program, you should
restste the K complexity of a system as the result of minimising the size of
the UTM and of the program.

Of course, you have set up the straw-man distinction between system and its
generator - the distinction between them is in our minds only (as with all
other distinctions). The computer is the program.
