I haven't read Maudlin proof but I can see that in the final analysis,
consciousness seems to be a characteristic relative to the beholder. A
self-referential perception. Whether the events supporting consciousness are
physically ordained, virtually ordained, in a movie or just accidental, as
long as they appear to be, to the conscious entity itself, rationally
connected between each other and to the consciousness itself (anthropic
requirement), then that consciousness will exist at that level, be it
physical, virtual, movie, or accidental.

Going beyond that, I think that consciousness is also level independent, just
as it is playback independent or time share independent and here is why:

Let's go back to the issue of consciousness implemented by a series of
disconnected accidental events. In the context of the MW, most connections
from one "frame" to another "frame" in the MW are accidental. However, we can
only be aware of those frames that are linked by what appears to be rational
links which support both consciousness and physical laws. Therefore, in a
sense ALL EVENTS ARE ACCIDENTAL AND CONSCIOUSNESS THRIVES IN THIS
ENVIRONMENT.

Even without the comp hypothesis humans are (at least) Universal Turing

Nevertheless, I'm not sure rationality is necessary for consciousness.

>GSLevy:
>Similarly recording the activities of neurons and then playing them back
>results in no consciousness at playback time but consciousness certainly
>exists at recording time.

You say that, I guess, because the "activities of the neuron" are
counterfactually correct (sensible to environment change). And the
playback is only accidentally correct.
The problem is the following one. As Maudlin has shown, you can
transform an accidentally correct computation into a counterfactually
correct one WITHOUT adding any physical activity. (And this is what I do
with the filmed graph argument, the *crackpot proof* in Mallah's term).
This make the attribution of consciousness to the "physical recording"
problematic.
