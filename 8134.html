I didn't mean to imply substance dualism. Of course I agree that higher
level concepts like mind are completely dependent on lower level physics. A
'mental cause' is not something separate from physical causes. But this does
not mean that the higher level kind of causality is *not real* Just because
the high level kind of causality (mental causation) is completely
*dependent* on lower level physics, doesn't mean that the mental kind of
causality is necessarily completely *reducible* to lower level physics. In
dealing with mental concepts, I think one is dealing with a higher level of
description which for full explanation requires the positing of new
properties not completely reducible to low level physics. Again though, I'm
not suggesting that mental concepts are separate from physical concepts.
