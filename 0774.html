This is the Omega Point suggested by Frank Tipler. I find no appeal in
Tipler's idea. His concept of time is linear. According to his implicit
assumption, each world has an independent time line and all worlds eventually
converge to the same state. This viewpoint shortchange the richness of the
MWI in which time can flow in an ever branching and merging network, and in
which the links are constrained by anthropically enforced rationality (or
computationality to satisfy the computationalists). Tipler's Omega point is
only one of the many possible future that awaits us, but it is by no means
the only one. Tipler starts with a more or less reasonable premice, but then
extrapolate wildly.

Tipler defines the "Omega Point" as a state that this universal intelligence
will reach in the limit, after a subjectively infinite time. The "Omega
Point" is endowed with "personal" god-like powers of being omniscient,
omnipotent and good. And it will resurrect the dead. And the resurrected dead
will be cured of whatever disease or physical handicaps they had when they
were living. And Tipler identifies the Holy Ghost with the universal
wave-function. From this he justifies the strong anthropic principle by
design. Clearly, Tipler shoe-horns his own brand of religious belief into his
Omega Point theory even though he denies it.

George Levy

You can no longer detect curvature yet it is still there. The circumference of the circle never reaches that extreme of being perfectly straight which you can imagine exists between the circle in front of you and an identical one behind you. Now imagine two points along the curved line of the circle in front of you, then imagine them expanding away from one another at the speed of light. What have you got?
