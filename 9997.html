Jesse;

while I fully support your objection to 1Z's

"natural laws are the same in all naturally possible worlds."
and
"Well, it's hard to imagine pain being anything other
than painful!"

I question your restrictions in:

>any world that does not contain a logical or mathematical
> impossibility, or any other type of incoherence in its >description, is
> viewed as a possible world.<

These criteria are spring from our human views and IMO 'nature' (or whatever
you like to call 'everything') has the right to implement ideas, qualia,
processes (if I may restrict the subjects to those we can think of - ha-ha)
way beyond our measures of any allowability, coherence or description.

Well: IMO possible is possible, not just OK to 'our' rules.

John
