You don't have to decide who's unbiased. But if you refuse to consider some evidence because it might conflict with your opinion while you accept other evidence of the same type because it supports your opinion, then you are guilty of bias. And if you're going to act on your opinion in a way that affects others, then I think you have an ethical duty to consider the evidence.
