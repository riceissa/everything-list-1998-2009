With finite set of numbers say 1-100, if my sampling procedure generates a
selection index with a uniform distribution over the interval 1-100, then the
number being sampled will also have a uniform distribution INDEPENDENTLY of
the way the numbers are sorted in the interval.

However, with an infinite set of natural numbers, how can I have a selection
procedure with a uniform distribution? As an exercise, please come up with an
algorithm that could produce a uniformly distributed pseudo-random selection
index where the index ranges over all the natural number. In addition come up
with a procedure that generates a uniformly distributed pseudo-random sorting
of all the natural number. What would the first three numbers of a
"randomly selected" sequence look like?
:-)

And even if you could come up with an algorithm for generating a random
number from among all the natural numbers, any finite number that it would
produce would necessarily be biased toward zero since there are more numbers
above it then under it. Since the sampling is biased toward zero, the
ordering of the number is critical in defining the distribution.

Taking polls of finite population is different than taking polls of infinite
populations! For example in the set of natural numbers we can show that the
number of even numbers is equal to the number of multiples of three.
