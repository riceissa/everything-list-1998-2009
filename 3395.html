It seems unlikely that it could be otherwise. Presumably the impulse to make a decision has to originate from a lower level,
assuming that consciousness is supported by layers of unconscious processing? However the decisions in question were to do with when
to perform a simple action - pressing a key, or something similar. What about conscious decisions that are arrived at by evaluating
evidence, weighing possibilities, etc? Presumably they are also supported by unconscious layers which know how to evaluate evidence
etc... Surely the feeling of free will comes from us not being aware of these underlying processes.

Charles
