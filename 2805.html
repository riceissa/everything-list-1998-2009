>What's a consistent computational extension?

Take an incomplete theory T. Suppose that -p is not provable in
the theory, then you can add p, as new axiom, without being able
to prove -p. This means p
is consistent with the theory. In particular the theory T+p is
a consistent extension of the theory T. Now a consistent computational
extension is a consistent extension where p is DU-accessible.
p is generated by a computation. It will be translated (in arithmetic)
by -Bew(-p) with p sigma_1.
