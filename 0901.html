Sigh.

Calling something "patently absurd" without giving specific evidence of
absurdity (i.e., logical contradiction or experimental falsification) is
hardly how science should be practiced. Everett's original
relative-sate idea was dismissed by many physicists of the day simply on
the grounds that it lead to "absurdities," which just meant conclusions
that, because of subjective bias, they found uncongenial.
