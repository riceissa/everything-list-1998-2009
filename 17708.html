On 28 Aug, 21:30, John Mikes <jami....domain.name.hidden> wrote:

> *Is the 'hard problem' and 'outside' factor? Maybe, for the closed inventory
> we have in today's conventional sciences. Our interpretations are temporary,
> as I call it (after Colin H) our "perceived reality" (of today), but
> different from the ancient formulations - without 'stepping outside
> ourselves into nowhere'. Soi-disant or else.*

Yes, when all is said and done, we must be humble in the face of
posterity. Particularly we must trying to realise (impossible though
this may be to envision) how utterly different things will look after
a paradigm shift of the magnitude of the one that precipitated the
modern mechanistic age. Will the next age be computational? Or
something entirely unexpected? Personally, if I had my wish, I'd want
us to rediscover some of that lost pre-mechanistic enchantment, but in
some manner that doesn't jettison the genuinely empowering insights of
the intervening phases. Nonetheless, when I contemplate the current
quality of 'insight' of science so far as mentality is concerned I'm
irresistibly reminded of Groucho Marx's querulous entreaty "Who are
you going to believe - me, or your own eyes?"

David
