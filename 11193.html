George Levy:

The scientist could prove that he is not alone by invoking the principle of
sufficient reason: nothing is arbitrary and exist with no reason. If
something exists in a particular arbitrary way (himself) with no reason for
him to be in that particular way, then all other alternatives of him must
also exist (the Plenitude). Hence he is not alone. Solipsism is dead.

George
